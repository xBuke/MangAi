Best AI tools for businesses in Croatia in 2025

Updated: January 28, 2025 · MangAi · 8 min read

Artificial intelligence is no longer just a topic for tech conferences – it has become a practical tool that Croatian businesses are increasingly using to save time, boost productivity, and communicate better with clients. In this article we present tools that are available on the Croatian market or easily applicable in a local context.

Why AI tools make sense for Croatian businesses

Small and medium-sized businesses in Croatia often work with limited budgets and smaller teams. AI tools allow the same people to get more done without hiring additional staff – which is especially important in a region where specialists are costly and hard to find.

Language support and local context

Many global AI tools now support Croatian or can be adapted easily. The key is to choose tools that understand context – not just translation, but also the cultural specifics of communicating with Croatian customers.

Compliance and data in the EU

Since Croatia is in the EU, it is important to use solutions that respect GDPR and keep data within the European Union where possible. This reduces legal risk and gives clients greater assurance.

Categories of tools worth considering

There is no single “best” AI tool – it depends on what the business wants to automate. In practice, three groups are used most often.

Text and content generation

Tools for writing emails, social posts, product descriptions, or even long-form documentation save hours per week. It is important that a human always reviews the output – AI is an assistant, not a replacement for strategy and brand voice.

Business process automation

From sending reminders to generating reports or updating the CRM – workflow automation reduces manual work and errors. Croatian businesses often use no-code/low-code platforms that integrate easily with existing systems.

Analytics and decisions

AI can analyze sales data, user behaviour, or market trends and suggest next steps. This is especially helpful for smaller teams that don’t have a dedicated data analytics function. Example of a simple workflow in no-code tools:

New inquiry → AI classification → Response (template) → Human review → Send

How to start without too much risk

We recommend starting with one clear use case – e.g. automatic replies to common inquiries or monthly summary generation. Once that process is in place and shows value, you can add more areas.
